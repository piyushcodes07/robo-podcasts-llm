Trees are crucial for maintaining soil and water cycles—removing them erodes soil and disrupts water availability, degrading habitats further. Forests house diverse species, and their destruction removes shelter, food, and water needed for survival. Continuous forest loss fuels a biodiversity crisis affecting the planet.

Warming causes droughts, increasing forest fire risks, releasing more carbon. It's self-perpetuating. As climate warms, ecosystems stress, making forests less resilient to pests and diseases, degrading them further. This feedback loop leads to drastic climate pattern changes, making the planet hotter and less diverse. Breaking this cycle requires aggressive action.
